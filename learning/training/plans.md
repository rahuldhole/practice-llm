# Training plans

### I. The "From Scratch" Paradigms (Foundational)

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. [Dense Pre-training](I_foundational/dense_pretraining.md)** | Use when building a custom foundation model from scratch with a large budget ($50k+) and proprietary data. Ensures maximum scaling stability. | **$25,000+** (Full run)<br>~$3,200 (Spot compute) | **Dataset Range:** 150B–300B tokens.<br>For a 3B parameter model at Chinchilla-optimal scale: $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 150\text{B} = 2.7 \times 10^{21}$ FLOPs. On 8x H100 node (350 TFLOPS/GPU active) $\approx 267$ hrs. Compute cost at $1.50/hr/GPU = 267 \times 8 \times 1.50 \approx \$3,200$. Setup/storage overhead brings the real budget to \$25k+. |
| **2. [Sparse (MoE) Pre-training](I_foundational/sparse_moe_pretraining.md)** | Use when you need the intelligence of a large model (8B+) but have a strict inference speed/compute budget (active params <2B). | **$5,000+** (Full run)<br>~$1,500 (Spot compute) | **Dataset Range:** 100B–200B tokens.<br>For 3B total / 800M active params model: $6 \times 800\text{M} \times 150\text{B} = 7.2 \times 10^{20}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 71$ hrs. Compute cost: $71 \times 8 \times 1.50 \approx \$850$. Add 30% latency penalty for inter-expert token routing (`all-to-all` sync) $\approx \$1,100$. Real-world multi-node cluster/storage overhead brings it to \$5k+. |
| **3. [Long-Context Pre-training](I_foundational/long_context.md)** | Use when adapting a model to handle huge documents, entire codebases, or complex multi-turn chats natively. | **$1,500+** | **Dataset Range:** 10B–20B context-extended tokens (e.g. 32K context).<br>Attention overhead increases compute requirements by ~1.5x: $1.5 \times 6 \times 3\text{B} \times 20\text{B} = 5.4 \times 10^{20}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 53$ hrs. Compute cost: $53 \times 8 \times 1.50 \approx \$636$. Total budget with setup: \$1,500+. |
| **4. [Tokenizer-First Training](I_foundational/tokenizer_bespoke.md)** | Use when working with highly specialized syntax (logs, chemical formulas, code). Standard tokenizers fragment these, wasting context window. | **$100 - $500** | **Dataset Range:** 10GB–50GB text (approx. 2B–10B tokens).<br>SentencePiece training takes ~5–10 hrs on multi-core CPU/single GPU (~$20). Vocab embedding alignment: 100M tokens on 1x H100 for 12 hrs: $12 \times 1.50 \approx \$18$. Total: under \$100-\$500. |

### II. Adaptation Paradigms (The "Moat" Builders)

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5. [Continual Pre-training (CPT)](II_adaptation/continual_pretraining.md)** | Use when you need to inject deep domain knowledge (legal, medical, corporate docs) into an already smart base model. Saves 95%+ compute vs. scratch. | **$150 - $1,000** | **Dataset Range:** 1B–5B tokens.<br>For 3B model: $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 5\text{B} = 9 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 9$ hrs. Compute cost: $9 \times 8 \times 1.50 \approx \$108$ compute. Total budget including data pipeline setup: \$150-\$1,000. |
| **6. [Model Merging (Frankenmerging)](II_adaptation/frankenmerging.md)** | Use when you want to merge separate capabilities (e.g., coding + creative writing) instantly with zero training cost. | **$0** | **Dataset Range:** None (uses existing checkpoint weights directly).<br>Linear or spherical weight interpolation (Slerp/TIES/DARE) runs in CPU RAM inside 10–30 mins. Compute cost: \$0. |
| **7. [Downcycling](II_adaptation/downcycling_moe.md)** | Use when upgrading a highly-capable Dense model to a faster MoE architecture, avoiding expensive random-init pre-training. | **$500 - $2,000** | **Dataset Range:** 5B–10B tokens (for gating/expert stabilization).<br>Upgrading 3B Dense $\rightarrow$ 3B MoE (800M active): $6 \times 800\text{M} \times 10\text{B} = 4.8 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 5$ hrs. Compute cost: $5 \times 8 \times 1.50 \approx \$60$. Setup and hyperparameter tuning sweeps: \$500-\$2,000. |
| **8. [Knowledge Distillation](II_adaptation/knowledge_distillation.md)** | Use when targeting edge/mobile deployments. Transfers teacher log probabilities to achieve outsized reasoning power in a lightweight model. | **$1,000 - $5,000** | **Dataset Range:** 10B–30B tokens (synthetic / teacher-annotated).<br>Student (3B) learns from Teacher (70B+). Total distillation compute footprint is $\approx 2\text{x}$ standard training due to teacher inference forward pass: $2 \times 6 \times 3\text{B} \times 20\text{B} = 7.2 \times 10^{20}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 71$ hrs $\approx \$850$. Total budget: \$1,000-\$5,000. |

### III. Alignment & Preference Paradigms (Post-Training)

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **9. [SFT (Supervised Fine-Tuning)](III_alignment/supervised_finetuning.md)** | First post-training step to transition a base pre-trained model into an instruction-following assistant. Teaches formatting and prompt response. | **$50 - $500** | **Dataset Range:** 50,000–100,000 QA pairs (approx. 50M–100M tokens).<br>For 3B model: $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 100\text{M} = 1.8 \times 10^{18}$ FLOPs. 1x H100 $\approx 1.5$ hrs. Compute cost: $1.5 \times 1.50 \approx \$2.25$. Setup and data cleaning: \$50-\$500. |
| **10. [DPO (Direct Preference Optimization)](III_alignment/direct_preference_opt.md)** | Use when aligning model tone, personality, or safety guardrails. Eliminates the need to train a separate reward model (much more stable than PPO). | **$100 - $1,000** | **Dataset Range:** 10,000–20,000 preference pairs (approx. 20M–40M tokens).<br>For 3B model: requires forward pass on active and reference models $\approx 9 \times 3\text{B} \times 40\text{M} = 1.08 \times 10^{18}$ FLOPs. 1x H100 $\approx 1$ hr $\approx \$1.50$. Setup and evaluations: \$100-\$1,000. |
| **11. [ORPO (Odds Ratio Preference Optimization)](III_alignment/odds_ratio_preference.md)** | Use to speed up alignment and prevent the model from losing critical token probabilities during disjoint SFT and preference phases. | **$100 - $800** | **Dataset Range:** 20,000 preference pairs (approx. 40M tokens).<br>SFT and preference loss computed simultaneously in one step: $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 40\text{M} = 7.2 \times 10^{17}$ FLOPs. 1x H100 $\approx 40$ mins $\approx \$1.00$ compute. |
| **12. [KTO (Kahneman-Tversky Optimization)](III_alignment/kahneman_tversky_opt.md)** | Use when collecting thumbs-up/down feedback from production users. Easier to harvest than structured pairwise preferences. | **$100 - $800** | **Dataset Range:** 30,000 binary feedback labels (approx. 30M tokens).<br>Aligns single outcomes: $9 \times 3\text{B} \times 30\text{M} = 8.1 \times 10^{17}$ FLOPs. 1x H100 $\approx 45$ mins $\approx \$1.10$ compute. |
| **13. [RLHF (PPO/GRPO)](III_alignment/reinforcement_learning_ppo.md)** | Use for complex logical tasks (math, coding, reasoning loops) where outcomes can be validated by rules or programmatic verifiers. | **$1,000 - $5,000** | **Dataset Range:** 10,000 prompt dataset (multiple rollout runs).<br>For a 3B model using GRPO (actor, reference, rollouts, reward models): $\approx 4\text{x}$ standard compute footprint $\approx 4 \times 6 \times 3\text{B} \times 10\text{M} = 7.2 \times 10^{17}$ FLOPs per epoch. 8x H100 node for 10–20 hrs $\approx \$120-\$240$. Total cost with convergence runs: \$1,000-\$5,000. |

### IV. Efficiency Paradigms (Resource-Constrained)

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **14. [PEFT (LoRA/QLoRA)](IV_efficiency/peft_lora_qlora.md)** | Use when training on restricted hardware (single GPU) or when deploying thousands of task-specific adapters for multi-tenant apps. | **$10 - $100** | **Dataset Range:** 10,000–50,000 samples (approx. 10M–50M tokens).<br>Updates <1% parameters; Base model frozen. Runs on local 1x RTX 4090 (24GB VRAM) at \$0.30/hr for 5 hrs $\approx \$1.50$. |
| **15. [Q-Fine-Tuning](IV_efficiency/q_finetuning.md)** | Use to fit training of large models (e.g., 70B) onto accessible consumer/indie hardware by compressing parameter representation. | **$10 - $100** | **Dataset Range:** 10,000 samples (approx. 10M tokens).<br>Weights frozen in 4-bit/8-bit; only small adapter weights are trained. Runs on 1x RTX 4090 for 2 hrs $\approx \$0.60$ compute. |
| **16. [Adapter Training](IV_efficiency/adapter_bottlenecks.md)** | Use when modular plug-and-play capability is required at runtime without modifying or altering the underlying base model parameters. | **$20 - $150** | **Dataset Range:** 20,000 samples (approx. 20M tokens).<br>Trains small bottleneck layers added to frozen base. Runs on 1x RTX 4090 for 6 hrs $\approx \$1.80$ compute. |

### V. Data-Centric Paradigms (The "Creative" Moats)

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **18. [Synthetic Data Scaling](V_datacentric/synthetic_data_scaling.md)** | Use when human-labeled data is scarce, expensive, or hard to scale. Allows you to train a smaller model to near-frontier reasoning performance. | **$500 - $3,000** | **Dataset Range:** 1M–10M generated prompt-response tokens.<br>OpenAI/Anthropic API generation: 10M tokens $\times$ \$5.00/1M tokens $\approx \$50$ API cost. Custom self-distillation (Qwen-72B on Vast.ai): 8x H100 run for 20 hrs generating data $\approx \$240$ compute. |
| **19. [Curriculum Learning](V_datacentric/curriculum_learning.md)** | Use for highly logical and structured tasks (math, coding). Ramping up difficulty systematically prevents gradient confusion and speeds convergence. | **$100 - $500** | **Dataset Range:** 50,000 samples sorted by difficulty (approx. 50M tokens).<br>Difficulty sorting (perplexity/length check on CPU) $\approx \$0$. 3B model training on 50M tokens: 1x H100 for 1.5 hrs $\approx \$2.25$. Setup overhead: \$100-\$500. |
| **20. [Reflection/Self-Correction Training](V_datacentric/self_correction_reflection.md)** | Use to build reliable reasoning models. Teaches the model to debug its own logic prior to spitting out the final answer. | **$500 - $2,500** | **Dataset Range:** 50M base tokens, expanded to 250M tokens by adding multi-step thinking traces.<br>For 3B model: $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 250\text{M} = 4.5 \times 10^{18}$ FLOPs. 1x H100 $\approx 3.5$ hrs $\approx \$5.25$. Curation/generation of step-by-step traces: \$500-\$2,500. |
| **21. [Multi-Modal Injection](V_datacentric/multimodal_projection.md)** | Use when adding image, audio, or video capabilities to an existing text model, avoiding training the entire system from scratch. | **$500 - $2,000** | **Dataset Range:** 1M image-text pairs (approx. 500M tokens equivalent).<br>Phase 1 (Align projector): $6 \times 20\text{M (projector)} \times 500\text{M} = 6 \times 10^{16}$ FLOPs. Phase 2 (Joint fine-tuning on 2B tokens): $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 2\text{B} = 3.6 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. 4x H100 $\approx 7$ hrs $\approx \$42$. Data pipeline/infra setup: \$500-\$2,000. |

### VI. Architectural Modification Paradigms

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **22. [Weight Pruning](VI_architectural/weight_pruning.md)** | Use when optimizing a model for faster inference speeds and smaller disk footprints by discarding less-activated channels or weights. | **$100 - $1,000** | **Dataset Range:** 1B tokens recovery dataset.<br>Sparsity calculation $\approx \$0$. Fine-tuning pruned model (1.5B parameters active) on 1B tokens: $6 \times 1.5\text{B} \times 1\text{B} = 9 \times 10^{18}$ FLOPs. 1x H100 $\approx 7$ hrs $\approx \$10.50$. |
| **23. [Depth-to-Width Conversion](VI_architectural/depth_width_conversion.md)** | Use to tune a model architecture for specific hardware compute-to-bandwidth ratios (e.g., sequential scaling bottlenecks). | **$500 - $2,500** | **Dataset Range:** 5B tokens stabilization dataset.<br>Converting 3B model (e.g., layer expansion): stabilization tuning $\approx 6 \times 3\text{B} \times 5\text{B} = 9 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 9$ hrs $\approx \$108$ compute. |
| **24. [Activations Tuning](VI_architectural/activation_tuning.md)** | Use when fine-tuning a model for optimal latency throughput or when seeking slight training stability gains from activation behavior. | **$200 - $1,000** | **Dataset Range:** 2B tokens stabilization dataset.<br>Adjusting layers (e.g. GeLU $\rightarrow$ SwiGLU) + stabilization tuning: $6 \times 3\text{B} \times 2\text{B} = 3.6 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. 4x H100 node $\approx 7$ hrs $\approx \$42$ compute. |

### VII. Operational/Infrastructure Paradigms

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **25. [Federated Training](VII_operational/federated_training.md)** | Use when training on highly restricted or private data (medical logs, defense files) spread across remote clients that cannot share raw datasets. | **$1,000 - $5,000** | **Dataset Range:** 100M tokens per client (1B tokens total across 10 clients).<br>Network sync delays increase overall training duration by ~300%: $3.0 \times (6 \times 3\text{B} \times 1\text{B}) = 5.4 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. 10x local RTX 4090 GPUs running for 24 hrs $\approx \$150$ compute. Stack engineering/security setup: \$1,000-\$5,000. |
| **26. [Active Learning](VII_operational/active_learning_loops.md)** | Use when human annotation is extremely expensive. Saves labeling costs by only requesting labels for items the model is highly uncertain about. | **$1,000 - $10,000** | **Dataset Range:** 10M token pool, selecting 50,000 samples for manual labeling per loop iteration.<br>Compute cost for inference filtering: $\approx 10\text{M tokens} \times 2\text{x student parameters} \approx 6 \times 10^{16}$ FLOPs (~$5). Annotation cost dominates the budget: 50,000 samples $\times$ \$0.15/sample $\approx \$7,500$. |

### VIII. Edge & Local Hardware Optimization Paradigms (New Category)

| Paradigm | When to Train & Why (Use Cases) | Est. Cost (Indie/Spot) | Rough Calculations (Data & Compute) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **27. [Quantization-Aware Training (QAT)](VIII_edge/quantization_aware_training.md)** | Use when targeting extreme edge/mobile compression (INT4/INT2) where standard post-training quantization would cause catastrophic loss of model reasoning capabilities. | **$100 - $500** (Local run) | **Dataset Range:** 500M–1B tokens calibration subset.<br>For 3B model: QAT simulation via MLX or private node. $6.5 \times 3\text{B} \times 1\text{B} = 1.95 \times 10^{19}$ FLOPs. Local M2 Ultra / M3 Max (approx. 20 TFLOPS effective) $\approx 270$ hours. Power/local hardware cost $\approx \$100-\$500$. |
| **28. [Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) Drafting](VIII_edge/multi_token_prediction.md)** | Use when you want to train 1B–3B models to output multiple tokens in parallel at each step, massively boosting local decoding speed (tokens per second). | **$200 - $1,000** | **Dataset Range:** 10B–50B tokens.<br>Training with $K=2$ secondary heads adds ~30% projection overhead: $1.3 \times 6 \times 3\text{B} \times 10\text{B} = 2.34 \times 10^{20}$ FLOPs. 8x H100 node $\approx 23$ hours. Spot cost: $23 \times 8 \times \$1.50 \approx \$276$ compute. |
| **29. [NPU-Native Kernel Compilations](VIII_edge/npu_kernel_compilation.md)** | Use when compiling and optimizing model graphs to target local NPUs (Apple ANE, Qualcomm Hexagon, Intel Lunar Lake) to prevent execution latency bottlenecks. | **$0** (Developer rig) | **Dataset Range:** 1,000 performance test prompt samples.<br>Runs local model JIT/AOT lowering compilation scripts. Computes fusions and layout adjustments on host system within 1–2 hours. Compute cost: \$0. |
| **30. [Speculative Decoding Distillation](VIII_edge/speculative_decoding_distillation.md)** | Use when training a hyper-lightweight drafter model (<100M) to match the token distribution of your main model, enabling speculative decoding speeds via llama.cpp/Ollama. | **$100 - $500** | **Dataset Range:** 1B–2B tokens (teacher-labeled logits).<br>Student model training: $6 \times 100\text{M} \times 2\text{B} = 1.2 \times 10^{18}$ FLOPs. 1x RTX 4090 GPU (80 TFLOPS effective) $\approx 4.2$ hours. Compute cost: $4.2 \times \$0.30 \approx \$1.26$. Setup and target dataset logging: \$100-\$500. |
